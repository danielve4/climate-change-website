Climate Change

What is climate change?

Climate is the average or typical weather in a region. Countries near the equator have a climate that is warm while countries near the North or South Pole have a climate that is cooler. Climate change is the change in Earthâ€™s overall climate due to an increase in temperature. The planet is warming and it is being caused by human related activities.

The Impact on You

Climate change affects every person in the world and will affect our children and their children to follow. People in America are noticing longer summers with higher temperatures and shorter winters. Others are experiencing heavier rainfalls that are causing floods more often than before.

Causes of Climate Change

Signs of climate change are becoming more abundant in the atmosphere and in the oceans. There are increasing concentrations of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere due to the burning of coal, oil, and gas. The rise in carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is preventing the heat from the sun from leaving Earth, thus affecting the temperatures all over the world.

Excessive Heat

Due to the excess heat that the carbon dioxide is preventing from being released, more regions are facing droughts. Not only are they facing droughts, but they are also facing an elevated risk of wildfires. Besides being dangerous, wildfires pollute the environment with their smoke which also accelerates climate change.

Furthermore, the excessive can also increase the amount of heat-related illnessess such as heat stokes, heat cramps, heat rashes, and heat exhaustion.

Agriculture Sector Affected

The United States alone has an agricultural industry producing an estimated $330 billion worth of agricultural commodities. The agricultural systems used in the industry rely heavily on climate to keep production going. This means that negative impacts on the climate is endangering the agricultural industry in the United States. In addition to affecting our economy, we are in danger of producing less food for the ever-growing population.

Ocean Life Threatened

Not only is the carbon dioxide preventing heat from escaping but it is also interacting with ocean water and making it more acidic (figure 1). This in turn makes the water more corrosive and prevents life forms with shells or skeletons from growing and reproducing normally. The danger these life forms face will affect the marine food chain.

What You Can Do

Inform Others

You can start by spreading the word about climate change. Not enough people are aware that human activity is the cause of our planet warming. A Pew Study conducted in 2014 concluded that about half of the elderly surveyed disagree that scientist think the Earth is getting warmer due to human activities (figure 2).

Other Transportation Methods

Another action you can take is using other methods of transportation. Instead of navigating around with a car, you can ride a bike or ride the local public transportation system. Not only does this decrease the amount of traffic in the road but also reduced the amount of pollution coming from vehicles that is strengthening climate change.
